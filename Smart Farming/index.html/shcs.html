Soil Health Card Scheme

Description: Launched by the Government of India in 2015, the Soil Health Card Scheme provides farmers with detailed reports on the nutrient status of their soil to help make informed decisions about fertilization and crop selection.
Benefits: Farmers receive soil health cards with recommendations on nutrient management, crop planning, and use of organic matter. It helps improve soil fertility and crop productivity sustainably.
Eligibility: All farmers across India are eligible for this scheme, regardless of landholding size. The cards are updated every two years.
How to Apply: Soil samples are collected by agricultural officials or farmers themselves. Applications can be made through local agriculture offices or the official portal: soilhealth.dac.gov.in
